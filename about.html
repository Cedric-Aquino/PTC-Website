ABOUT PTC

Pateros Technological College (PTC) is a technical-vocational institution founded on January 29, 1993, through Municipal Ordinance No. 93-07. It officially began operations on August 16, 1993, initially offering short-term programs and two-year associate degrees in Computer Science, Computer Secretarial Science, and Computer Technology. Early in its development, PTC partnered with Systematrix Computer Education and Services, Inc. (SCESI) under Municipal Resolution No. 64-95, which authorized the Municipality of Pateros to enter into a Memorandum of Agreement with the organization.

However, the partnership between PTC and SCESI concluded in October 1995. Committed to sustaining its technical-vocational mission, PTC established a new collaboration with the Technological University of the Philippines (TUP). On September 26, 1995, PTC became a beneficiary of TUP’s Adopt-A-School Program through another Memorandum of Agreement. This partnership led to the introduction of the institution’s first four-year Bachelor of Computer Science program in the 1997–1998 academic year. Additionally, PTC served as a partner institution for the off-campus training of TUP’s undergraduate and graduate students.

Through this collaboration, PTC strengthened its foundation and gradually achieved institutional independence. This progress enabled the college to offer ladderized programs leading to bachelor’s degrees. In the 2006–2007 school year, it introduced the Bachelor of Science in Education, majoring in Information Systems with a minor in Mathematics. The following year, PTC expanded its offerings to include a Certificate in Hotel and Restaurant Management, which leads to a Bachelor of Science in Hospitality Management, as well as a Bachelor of Science in Office Administration.

2-Year Program

Bachelor of Science Information Technology
(BSIT)

The emphasis of this four-year degree is on technology and computers. Its main goal is to prepare students for the rapidly changing demands of the IT sector by teaching them the fundamentals of computer hardware, software, databases, algorithms, telecommunications, user strategies, web development, application testing, and computer graphics. As a result, when applying for IT support business processes, be well-prepared.

Bachelor of Science in Office Administration
(BSOA)

Students enrolled in this four-year degree are prepared for a job in a professional, outcome-focused, high-tech setting. The program includes courses that will fully acquaint students with office administration procedures, technology, and modern business setup.

Certificate in Computer Sciences
(CCS)

Under the Institute of Information and Communication Technology, this two-year ladderized curriculum is offered. Information technology, database management, and system maintenance are its key focus.

Certificate in Office Administration
(COA)

Within the Institute of Business and Office Administration, this two-year program is ladderized. Techniques for human resources and office management are its main objectives.

2-Year Program (PAYING/NON-UNIFAST)

Associate in Hotel and Restaurant Technology
(AHRT)

The Associate in Hotel and Restaurant Technology program cultivates skills in front desk management, food and beverage preparation, hotel and restaurant operations management, and housekeeping services. In order to guarantee excellent visitor experiences, it places a strong emphasis on hands-on training, superior customer service, and the incorporation of hospitality standards.

Through the integration of academic knowledge and practical applications, the program fosters creativity, professionalism, and ethical work practices while empowering students to adjust to the ever-changing demands of the hospitality sector.

Associate in Human Resource Development
(AHRD)

Students enrolled in the Associate in Human Resource Development gain the fundamental skills necessary to oversee workforce operations, such as hiring, training, performance reviews, and employee relations. It combines labor relations, organizational behavior, and management concepts to create a framework for efficient HR procedures.

The curriculum prepares students to assist HR operations in a variety of organizational settings by emphasizing ethical decision-making, problem-solving, and interpersonal communication.

Associate in Accounting Information System
(AAIS)

The two-year Associate in Accounting Information System (AAIS) curriculum equips students with fundamental knowledge and abilities in information systems and accounting. The goal of this multidisciplinary program is to help students manage and apply financial data in contemporary company settings by fusing technology and accounting principles. The curriculum prepares graduates for entry-level positions in data analysis, bookkeeping, accounting, and information systems support. They can also pursue a bachelor's degree in a related field.
